About

Hello, I'm Todd Nestor. This is a portfolio of some of the websites I have developed. If you'd like to see more of a resume you can go to my LinkedIn profile. I started out learning web development in 2000 when I was 13 years old. It started because I was at Wal-mart and saw a book titled, The Complete Idiot's Guide to Creating a Website (probably a much earlier version than this link is to) and asked my mom to buy it for me. I read the nearly 400 page book cover-to-cover that night. The next day after I got home from school I started building my first web page.

Not long after that I started learning JavaScript and then moved on to learn Perl to create my first interactive applications. Around 2005 I got a job as a web developer for a company called Aros.net and PHP and MySQL became my bread and butter. That company eventually went bankrupt, but I have continued to do freelance work throughout the years and am currently looking at getting back into web development full time and am looking for a position. That's not to say I don't freelance anymore, if you want to hire me for full-time, part-time, or freelance work, feel free to contact me.

I am always learning new things about the languages and technologies I already know, and am also always learning new languages and technologies. Besides web development, I also spent six years in the Army National Guard where I was trained as a Chinese linguist (comes in handy since my wife is Chinese) and an Intelligence Analyst. I spent nine months working as an analyst in Kosovo as part of the NATO Peacekeeping troops. When I'm not working I love hiking, biking, kayaking, reading, and watching movies.

Besides a former soldier and a web developer I am also a published author. The Foreign Language Press (a publisher in China) has published one article of mine (Meeting Nanjing:Ankara, Two Transportation Hubs) and I have two more pending publishing (both about Nanjing as well) and also a book (titled Next Stop Beijing).
